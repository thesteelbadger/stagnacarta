## How It Works

An institution is given a metric to measure its performance. Over time, the metric becomes the goal. The institution optimises for the number rather than the outcome the number was supposed to represent. When the metric and the outcome diverge — as they always eventually do — the institution follows the metric.

This is not gaming. Gaming implies conscious manipulation. Metric capture is structural: the institution's incentive structure, career progression, funding allocation, and political accountability all orient around the measurable output. The unmeasurable outcome becomes invisible.

## Where It Appears

**Natural England and the Bechstein's bat**: Population recorded at 1,500 in 2005, rising to 25,000 by 2011 after better survey methods. Natural England's response: the increase reflects better recording, not population growth, so conservation status shouldn't change. The metric (conservation status) was preserved against the data because revising it would require judgement.

**Ofwat and water leakage**: Sets leakage volume targets for water companies but doesn't mandate adoption of proven detection technology. Water companies hit the target number at minimum cost rather than deploying the best available solution. The measurement rewards reaching a number, not solving the problem.

**Part O window regulations**: Optimise for "zero falls from windows" — a metric capturing 0.8% of domestic accidents. The cost (darker homes, uglier buildings, delayed construction) falls on metrics nobody in the regulatory process was required to consider.

**CQC inspections**: Produces ratings without producing inspections — the output metric is maintained while the input activity collapsed 58%. The appearance of oversight is preserved; the substance has evaporated.

**The CSEW and burglary**: The Crime Survey has measured burglary with the same methodology since 1982. The society it measures has transformed — double-income households, declining response rates, excluded populations. The instrument is degrading but nobody recalibrates it because the numbers it produces are politically convenient.

## The Deeper Pattern

Metric capture is the institutional equivalent of teaching to the test. It produces institutions that are excellent at generating the required numbers and incapable of noticing when those numbers have disconnected from reality.

The correction mechanism — someone saying "this metric no longer measures what it was designed to measure" — requires judgement. Judgement requires someone to be accountable for a choice. The system selects against both.
