## Division of Labour

**Claude (AI)**: Research synthesis, data gathering, source finding, structural organisation, fact-checking, first-draft body pages for the reference layer (budget, headcount, governance), stress-testing arguments, devil's advocacy.

**Henry (human)**: Editorial voice, all introductions and conclusions, the editorial at the end of each failure entry, tradeoff assessments, and all content where a human tone is required.

The test: if a knowledgeable reader thinks "this person thinks differently from me" — success. If they think "well-prompted Claude output" — failure.

## Page Structure

Each page has visually distinct sections:

**Reference layer**: Factual, sourced, AI-assisted, openly labelled. Links to existing resources (Wikipedia, Institute for Government, GOV.UK) rather than duplicating. This layer can be mostly AI-generated and is openly acknowledged as such.

**Editorial layer**: Written by Henry, in his voice, with his name on it. Analysis, connections, controlled anger. This is the part that cannot be automated.

## Editorial Voice

Demonstrate anger through relentlessness of documentation, not adjectives. The reader arrives at outrage independently through accumulating evidence. Then one sentence of editorial voice lands like a hammer.

Wrong model: sustained shouting that validates existing believers. Right model: Orwell's essays, John Burn-Murdoch's FT threads — fury compressed into precision. Controlled detonation, not sustained shouting.

## External Critique Protocol

Periodically feed finished content to Gemini, ChatGPT, and other AI systems with instructions to argue against the thesis as strongly as possible. Compare outputs — differences between AI systems are informative.

Also periodically ask Claude to steelman the counter-thesis. Echo chamber risk is real: Claude arrives primed with Henry's framework, the source ecosystem has a tilt toward the stagnation thesis. External critique from systems with different training data is the best antidote.

## Comparative Discipline

Every mechanism documented on Stagnacarta must be tested against international comparators. If France has metric capture and Germany has accountability diffusion and the US has regulatory accretion, acknowledge it honestly. Where Britain is genuinely distinctive, show the evidence. Where it's not, say so and ask what the comparator did differently.

This is almost never done in UK policy media — the NHS is always compared to the US, never to the Dutch, Swiss, Australian, or Singaporean models. This comparative discipline inoculates against the strongest anti-declinist critique and makes the project more credible than anything else in the space.

## Statistics Discipline

When questioning official statistics, always offer alternative data sources that tell a different story. Metric capture applied to measurement systems is a specific analytical tool, not grounds for general scepticism. If you can point to insurance claims data, A&E assault presentations, or victim survey breakdowns — that's serious analysis. If it's just "it doesn't feel right" — that's not analysis. This can't always be done. Sometimes a metric is wrong but has no replacements. This is difficult.
