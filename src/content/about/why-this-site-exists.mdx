Each of the failures documented on this site is recorded somewhere. The SEND crisis has been reported by specialist charities. The university grade inflation scandal has been documented by academics and journalists. The planning system's dysfunction is a permanent feature of local newspaper coverage.

None of it has produced reform.

Part of the reason is institutional — the organs of the state are designed to resist the kind of systemic accountability that reform requires. But part of the reason is informational. Democratic accountability depends on common knowledge: not just that people know about a failure, but that they know their neighbours know too.

Fragmented media — everyone in their own algorithmic bubble, reading different things, seeing different stories — destroys the common knowledge that makes collective political pressure possible. The bat tunnel, SEND, equal value liability, and university inflation are connected. They are all expressions of the same underlying dysfunction: a state whose organs each optimise for their own metric with no reference to the systemic consequences, and a political culture that has built elaborate machinery for managing the appearance of governing without doing the substance of it.

Nobody has put them in the same place and made the connections visible. That is what this site is for.

It is an attempt to recreate, for systemic institutional failure, the common knowledge that fragmented media has destroyed. To make the pattern legible. To give the public the shared reference point from which outrage — and eventually reform — becomes possible.

It was built by one person with some domain knowledge, strong opinions, and genuine frustration, using AI to do the research synthesis and institutional mapping that would otherwise require a large team and significant funding. That combination — human judgement and lived experience, AI-assisted research and structure — is itself a demonstration that the barriers keeping serious independent policy analysis inside captured institutions are no longer insurmountable.

This site practises what it preaches about transparency: the methodology is open, the AI use is acknowledged, and the connections between failures are documented so they can be challenged. Nothing here is beyond dispute. Everything here is an invitation to debate.
