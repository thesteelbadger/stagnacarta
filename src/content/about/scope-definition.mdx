## In Scope

British state organs and their failures, specifically post-2008. The specific mechanisms by which siloed accountability produces systemic failure. The Blair/Brown institutional architecture (1997–2008) as baseline — what was built, and why it proved fragile. The austerity/post-2010 period as the era when institutional capacity was hollowed out.

Policy decisions coming down the track that British institutional dysfunction leaves the country ill-equipped to handle.

International comparisons — every mechanism tested against France, Germany, the US, or other comparators. Where Britain shares dysfunctions with comparable countries, we acknowledge honestly; where it is genuinely distinctive, we show the evidence. The comparative methodology is not a nice-to-have — it is the thing that makes this project analytically honest and distinguishes it from the 140-year declinist tradition.

Geopolitics where it has direct institutional hooks in Britain — AI, compute and energy (planning system, nuclear policy), Irish unification (GFA obligations, fiscal architecture), Islamism and Gulf funding (counter-terrorism policy, arms sales tradeoffs), multipolar disorder (defence posture, diplomatic capacity).

## Out of Scope

Geopolitical analysis for its own sake. International stories without a direct British institutional dimension.

The 140-year declinist tradition — Stagnacarta is not another chapter in the Tombs/Edgerton narrative of permanent British decline. It is a specific claim about post-2008 institutional failure — one that can be checked against the evidence and argued with.

## The Test

For every potential piece: does this illuminate a specific failure of British state organs to serve the public interest, or a specific policy decision where those organs are demonstrably ill-equipped? If not, it belongs elsewhere. Scope creep is the enemy of the site's credibility and analytical coherence. A site about everything is a site about nothing.
